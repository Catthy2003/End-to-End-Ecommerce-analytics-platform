### Executive Overview
During the analysis period, the business generated 13.59M BRL in total revenue from approximately 99K orders, serving 99K customers through nearly 3K sellers. A total of 135K order items were sold, indicating that many orders contained more than one product.

Revenue increased significantly from 2016 to 2017, before slightly declining in 2018. Despite the decline, 2018 remained substantially stronger than 2016, suggesting that the business maintained a larger customer base after its rapid expansion.

Why?

The sharp growth between 2016 and 2017 is consistent with rapid business expansion and customer acquisition. The slight decrease in 2018 could be attributed to partial-year data, seasonal effects, or a slowdown in demand rather than a structural decline.

### Customer Purchasing Behaviour
Quarterly purchasing activity shows that Quarter 4 recorded the highest revenue (4M), followed by Quarter 3 (3.52M). Quarter 1 and Quarter 2 recorded lower purchasing activity.

Revenue by weekday also indicates stronger customer activity during weekdays, particularly:

Monday: 2.23M BRL
Tuesday: 2.17M BRL
Wednesday: 2.11M BRL

Weekend revenue was noticeably lower, especially on Saturday and Sunday.

Customers appear to complete purchases primarily during the working week, possibly due to work routines or promotional campaigns launched on weekdays.

Quarter 4's stronger performance is likely associated with year-end shopping events and holiday purchasing behaviour.

What next?
Increase marketing investment before and during Quarter 4.
Launch weekday flash sales to capitalize on peak purchasing behaviour.
Design weekend promotions to improve conversion during lower-demand periods.
Sales & Product Performance

### Product Sales
The average customer purchased 1.14 items per order, indicating that most transactions consisted of a single product. The average product price was 120.65 BRL, while the Average Order Value reached 137.75 BRL. Average freight cost was 19.99 BRL.

Top-performing categories included:

Health Beauty (1.26M BRL)
Watches Gifts (1.21M BRL)
Bed Bath Table (1.04M BRL)

Why?

The relatively small difference between average product price and average order value suggests limited cross-selling, as customers typically purchase only one item per transaction.

High-performing categories likely reflect strong consumer demand and broad product availability.

What next?
Implement product bundle recommendations.
Introduce "Frequently Bought Together" suggestions.
Increase promotional investment in the highest-performing categories while reviewing low-performing categories for assortment optimization.

### Customer Satisfaction
Customer satisfaction remained strong, with an average review score of 4.03/5. More than half (53.63%) of all reviews awarded 5 stars, while only 14.91% were 1-star ratings.

Across most product categories, 5-star reviews consistently represented the largest share of customer feedback.

Why?

The high proportion of positive ratings suggests that customers were generally satisfied with both products and the purchasing experience.

Negative reviews likely reflect issues related to delivery, damaged products, or unmet customer expectations.

What next?
Investigate common reasons behind 1-star and 2-star reviews.
Combine review analysis with delivery performance to identify operational bottlenecks.
Apply sentiment analysis to review comments for deeper customer feedback insights.

### Pricing Distribution
The price distribution indicates that most products were concentrated within the lower price ranges, while only a small number of premium-priced products were sold.

Why?

The marketplace primarily serves mass-market consumers purchasing affordable products.

What next?
Segment customers by spending behaviour.
Evaluate opportunities to expand premium product offerings without affecting sales volume.
Personalize recommendations based on customer purchasing history.

### Executive Summary

Overall, the dashboard indicates a healthy e-commerce business with strong customer satisfaction, stable order values, and clear seasonality in purchasing behaviour. Revenue growth has been driven primarily by increasing order volume rather than higher spending per transaction. Product sales are concentrated in a small number of categories, and customer purchasing behaviour suggests opportunities to improve cross-selling and weekend sales performance. Future business efforts should focus on increasing basket size, strengthening customer retention, and leveraging seasonal demand through targeted marketing campaigns.
